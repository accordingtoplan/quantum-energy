# Quantum Prototype, Concept and Reference Research

## The play
The prototype is proof, not a website. It demonstrates: (1) MoreSleep understood the brief better than the client articulated it, (2) the new brand system works in the real world, (3) the deck strategy translates into something clickable. Scope: homepage + one PDP (PDP acts as landing page per client brief). Do not over-finish: ~85% polish, direction approval over detail nitpicking. Keep the name Quantum Energy Square, do not open the QTM naming question. The prototype is free; Figma system, real build, PDP suite, Amazon reskin, brand guidelines are the paid engagement.

## Concept
"Sustained energy by design, designed." The Working For You argument (the functional aisle is full of ingredients you can't feel; caffeine is the one you can) becomes the narrative spine of the site. Strategy becomes scroll.

One-line pitch for Dan and Leah: the category's product pages sell ingredients you take on faith. Quantum's is the first built around an effect you can feel, and it shows you the curve.

## Benchmark research findings (July 2026)
- Only ~49% of ecommerce sites deliver a good PDP experience; most leading sites rate mediocre or worse (Baymard). Benchmark status is achievable.
- Table stakes: large zoomable imagery, video (viewers ~144% more likely to add to cart), sticky mobile add-to-cart, benefit-led copy, full ingredient panel shown as a selling point (wellness top performers), reviews with filtering, speed (a 4-second load often bounces low-friction food purchases).
- UGC converts: visitors interacting with UGC convert ~161% higher; video testimonials ~80% uplift over text.
- 2026 design split: luxury restraint vs challenger maximalism; the middle loses. Quantum = challenger camp with credibility and maturity (Poppi energy, not Poppi tone).
- "Product is the demo": interactive product moments beat static grids.
- PDPs now serve three audiences: shoppers, search engines, AI shopping tools. Build structured data and Q&A blocks in from the start.

## Where Quantum leapfrogs (differentiators)
1. Energy curve module: spike-and-crash vs sustained line over a real day. No competitor has it. Signature move, shared homepage + PDP.
2. Moment-based selling: "when do you need it" selector (2pm slump primary, pre-workout, meetings, travel).
3. Ingredient panel as flex, not compliance: 100mg / 10g as graphic numbers, tappable ingredients ("what it does, whether you feel it").
4. Comparison with wit: routine vs Quantum, category archetypes, no competitor logos, composed challenger voice.
5. Answer-engine readiness: schema, machine-readable facts (talking point, not prototype feature).
6. Dessert-coded macro photography where the category shows granola.

## Headline work (verdict pending)
Construction: category word + outcome (per David). Candidates:
A. "Steady energy for unsteady days." (recommended; steady vs unsteady = spike-crash argument in four words; nobody in energy says "steady")
B. "Real caffeine. Real food. Real energy." (caffeine-forward variant)
Supporting: "Energy you can actually feel" moves to Block 2 as the thesis line. "No spike. No crash. No compromise." as recurring proof line. "The energy bar that actually has energy in it" reserved for campaign/social. "Caffeine with benefits" exists in client FAQ, usable as subline.
Rule: caffeine never in the headline (mechanism, not outcome) but always visible above the fold via subline: "The energy bar with 100mg clean caffeine + 10g protein. No spike, no crash."
