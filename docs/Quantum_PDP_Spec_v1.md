# Quantum Energy Square, PDP Specification v1
Working document. Mobile-first. One PDP template, all flavors slot in.
Reference logic: Rhode structure and calm, David repeated argument, Olipop flavor selector only, Magic Spoon macro playfulness, Graza personality within Shopify.

Taste notes locked: Rhode storytelling layers yes, Olipop overall too much, AG1 too clustered. Keep every block calm, one job per block.

---

## Block 0. Sticky Buy Bar (mobile)
Appears after scrolling past Block 1. Product name, flavor, price, Add to Cart.
Reference: wellness DTC standard, highest-ROI mobile element.
Rule: never more than four elements. Calm, not nagging.

## Block 1. Above the Fold, The Decision Zone
Left / top: gallery. Hero product shot (dessert-coded, dimensional), bite shot showing texture, in-hand scale shot, short loop video if available.
Right / below: product name, one-line outcome headline (not the product name repeated), star rating with review count, flavor selector, purchase module.

Flavor selector: Olipop mechanic. Visual chips showing each flavor, switching recolors accents and swaps gallery. One page, all flavors.

Purchase module: one-time vs Subscribe and Save toggle, savings stated in dollars and percent, quantity, Add to Cart, "Find us in store" as secondary CTA (retail tool logic, Walmart).

The three numbers appear here for the first time: 100mg clean caffeine. 10g plant protein. 0 crash.
Rule: everything needed to buy without scrolling. PDP as landing page.

## Block 2. The Argument, Stated Once, Clean
One full-width statement block. Expressive headline typography, no imagery.
Content: the category problem in two lines. Energy drinks spike and crash. Bars call sugar energy. Quantum is built around how caffeine actually works.
Reference: David's discipline, Rhode's whitespace.
This is the first storytelling layer. Short. The proof comes next.

## Block 3. The Energy Curve (signature module)
The benchmark move. Animated or scroll-driven curve: energy drink spike-and-crash line vs Quantum's sustained line, plotted across a real day (7am, 2pm, 6pm).
Copy: "Sustained energy by design." One supporting line on the mechanism: caffeine paired with macronutrients slows absorption, energy releases steadily.
Reference: none, that is the point. AG1 sells mechanism but clustered; we do it as one clean visual.
Rule: understandable in three seconds without reading.

## Block 4. The Three Numbers, Repeated
100mg, 10g, 0. Large graphic numerals, one line each.
100mg clean caffeine from organic green coffee, same as a cup of coffee.
10g plant-based protein.
0 crash, 0 artificial sweeteners, 0 seed oils.
Reference: David's macro wall, Magic Spoon's playful number treatment.
This is the argument's second repetition. It will repeat once more at the close.

## Block 5. Ingredient Flex
Full ingredient panel shown proudly, not collapsed. Each key ingredient tappable or hoverable: what it is, what it does, whether you feel it.
Certifications as a clean badge row: Non-GMO, Gluten-Free, Plant Based, Vegan.
Reference: wellness top performers show the full panel as a selling point. Rhode-level layout calm.
Voice: composed challenger. "Read the label. We insist."

## Block 6. The Moment Selector
Storytelling layer two. "When do you need it?" Three to five moments as a horizontal selector or tabs: 2pm slump (primary), pre-workout, between meetings, travel, on the trail.
Each moment: one image, one line, same product. Sells the routine, not the flavor.
Reference: no direct benchmark, derived from the habit-loop strategy. Rhode's routine content is the closest tonal reference.

## Block 7. Taste Block
Dessert-coded close-up, pastry chef story in two lines. Flavor descriptors, texture shot.
Purpose: resolve the "healthy bar tastes chalky" barrier. Looks like a treat, works like a system.
Reference: David bite shots, Bombon-level craveability, Graza personality in copy.

## Block 8. Comparison, With Wit
"Your current routine vs this." Energy drink and standard bar vs Quantum, three or four rows max: energy, crash, nutrition, fits in your pocket.
Voice: dry, composed, never punching down. No competitor logos, category archetypes only.
Reference: Proper Wild and Magic Spoon comparison tables, ours shorter and funnier.

## Block 9. Social Proof
Reviews (Yotpo), filterable. Lead with two or three highlighted quotes that mention the effect ("no crash", "carried me through the afternoon"), then the full list. UGC photos or short clips mixed in where available.
Reference: SKIMS density without clutter, "see why thousands made the switch" pattern from the brief.

## Block 10. Subscribe and Save Reprise
For scrollers who were not ready at the top. Restate savings, delivery flexibility, cancel anytime. One CTA.
Reference: standard DTC, kept to one screen.

## Block 11. FAQ, Short
Five to seven questions max: caffeine amount vs coffee, daily use, when to eat it, kids, allergens. Structured data (FAQ schema) for search and AI shopping tools.
Reference: Proper Wild's digestible FAQ pattern, trimmed.

## Block 12. The Close
The argument's third repetition, one line: "The functional aisle is full of ingredients you can't feel. This is the one you can."
Cross-sell: other flavors and variety pack, David-style partially cut-off grid so you sense there is more.
Footer with personality and email capture.

---

## Rules across all blocks
1. One job per block. If a block does two things, split or cut.
2. The argument appears exactly three times: Block 2, Block 4, Block 12. Never more.
3. Rhode calm is the default state, energy comes from type, color and the curve, not from density.
4. Every block must survive on a phone in one thumb-length.
5. Built as Shopify theme sections, each block editable by their team.

## Prototype scope
Fully built: Blocks 0-6, 8, 12.
Static mock within the page: Block 9.
Skipped or placeholder: 10, 11.

## Decisions locked (v1.1)
1. Flavor takeover: each flavor takes over the stage on switch. Full recolor of gallery, accents and backgrounds per flavor. Yellow remains the brand anchor at homepage and navigation level, the PDP becomes the flavor's world.
2. Energy curve: scroll-driven animation. The curve draws itself as you scroll, crash line falls, Quantum line holds.
3. Moment selector: interactive tabs in the prototype.
4. Pricing and subscription discount: placeholders until Katie Lee provides real values.
